About Haiti

Haiti is a country rich in history, culture, and traditions. Known for its vibrant art scene, lively festivals, and stunning natural landscapes, Haiti offers a unique experience for visitors and researchers alike.

Culture and Traditions

From the rhythmic beats of compas music to the colorful tapestries of vodou ceremonies, Haiti's culture is a vibrant tapestry of influences and traditions.

Food and Cuisine

Haitian cuisine is a blend of African, French, and indigenous influences. Discover traditional dishes like griot, akra, and pikliz.

Festivals and Events

Haiti hosts a variety of festivals throughout the year, including the famous Carnival and the Vodou Festival in Saut-d'Eau.

Natural Beauty

From the beaches of Labadee to the mountains of Kenscoff, Haiti's natural beauty is breathtaking and diverse.

Surface Information

Population: Approximately 11.1 million

Area: 27,750 square kilometers

Capital: Port-au-Prince

Official Languages: Haitian Creole, French
